Paperchromatography

first you would cut a peace of chromatographypaper to size and prepare a piece of paper, First make a pencil line at the bottem of the paper and place and lable the substances (E.g. I, II, III) Then you would have a dish that has the solvent and you would place the paper in some clamps at the top and then place just the bottem of the paper in the Solvent and let sit for a comproble amount of time. Then you would mesure the Solvent front and the distance from the pencil to the substance dots

RF Value

Next you would have to find the RF Value by dividing distance Travled by substance / distance travled by solvent

E.g.

A

3/25= 0,12

B

3/25= 0,12

C

10/25= 0,40

D

13/25= 0,52

E

18/25= 0,72

So the RF Value of each Dot is A=0,12; B=0,12; C=0,40; D=0,52; E=0,72

Steps

  1. Place a spot od each substance along a pencil line on the chromatographypaper. Lable each spot
  2. Stand the paper in the solvent while the pencil line is above the solvent. The solvent will rise up the paper. When it's near the top remove the paper allow it to dry.
  3. Mesure the distance travled by the difrent substances

The more soluble the substance the further the substance will travled

PaperChromaTography can be used for colourless substances by spraying or exposing the the chromatographypaper to locating agent.
